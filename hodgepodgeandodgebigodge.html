Latex Figures

The little doodles above were made programmatically in Latex. The source code is available in Overleaf. I'm pretty new to these, but it's kind of fun to break down a figure into lines, rectangles, circles, and coordinates. I made the bullseye doodle on this card for a first year physics lab I wrote exploring uncertainties (and distinguishing between "accuracy" and "precision"). *`Last edit: 2021/03/03`*

The Spread of Covid 19

In April 2020 I wrote a program to visualize the spread of COVID 19. I wrote this in Matlab, which ultimately meant that I had to post static videos, rather than allowing users to vary parameters and play with the model themselves. (This was the catalyst for me learning Dash.) It's still not user-friendly, and I dropped it before comparing to real-world scenarios, but perhaps the website is interesting to peruse nonetheless. *`Last edit: 2020/04/08`*
